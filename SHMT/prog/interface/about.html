The Sanskrit - Hindi Anusaaraka cum Anuvaadaka is a tool to assist a reader in understanding a Sanskrit text. The anuvaadaka though produces a translation, many a times the output is not correct, or is un-intellegible. In such cases, Anusaaraka guides the reader through various stages of analysis. A reader who is ready to take some extra efforts in understanding the text has all the detailed analysis of the text available to him, and based on the context, a reader can then interpret the text.

The analysis is done following the standard steps in Indian Grammatical tradition viz.

  • Padapaatha
  • Pada paricaya
  • samasta pada viccheda
  • samasta pada paricaya
  • vaakya vishleshana following the aakaanksha and sannidhi
  • artha nirdhaarana (to be added)
English-Hindi anusaaraka is an example of a practical demonstration of application of traditional shastras to solve contemporary problems.
